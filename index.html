Kirby's Adventure

by: Michael Yeh

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

When growing up, some of my favorite games are platformer games. For example, I played a lot of Super Mario Bros when it came out on the WII. Now I also enjoy playing Super Mario Bros Deluxe on the switch with my family. Therefore, I made a platformer game.

2. What I Learned

My parents told me to add a scoring system to the game so people can challenge themselves to get the best score. Therefore, I made a death counter that displays at the ending screen. My sister gave me the advice of changing the character to a cuter one. Originally I had a ninja, but in the end, I chose Kirby.

3. Architecture of My Game

My game has 5 classes, Jumper, Platforms, Spikes, Player, and Star. Jumper class is where all the functions are being ran and controls all the other classes. My Platforms and Spikes are similar, they draw rectangles and when player collides with platforms, it affects the player's x and y. When it collides with spikes, it redraws the player to the respawn point.

4. Technical Implementation

I am most proud of the mechanics of the game. I did a little research on some of the build in variables like Gravity and velocity. That took most of the time from making this game. Some problems I faced during these steps was that the background did not redraw so the cube leaves a trail behind it. I fixed it with a simple repaint(); function but it took really long to figure out.

5. Most Challenging Part

The most challenging part of this project was also the mechanics of the game. For one, I faced the background refreshing problem. The second problem I faced was how to get the player to respawn. Originally I tried to store the respawn points in an array but it was too complicated. So I made a certain respawn point for each level and used if else statement to test the level they are on to set certain respawn points.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

In the future, I would improve my game by having larger levels. The screen/background moves as the character moves around the level. I could also add enemies that spawn randomly and walks around just like the player does. If I were to take a step further, I could add a player 2 to the game.
